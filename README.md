# Ologosophy

**A Wholly Integrated View of Reality**

Ologosophy (o-lo-GOS-ophy) is a philosophical framework that integrates rationality, mind, morality, and artificial intelligence under a unified metaphysical ground. The framework takes the *Logos* as the ground of the triadic architecture of reality established by the Triadic Reality Model, where the ground is specified by its functional roles rather than by a specific metaphysical articulation of its character.

## Core Thesis

The founding paper argues that contemporary philosophy treats rationality, mind, morality, and AI as largely distinct domains, and that four minimal commitments most working inquirers already hold (that reality is rational, informational, dynamic, and discoverable) require the integrated framework this program develops.

Six interrelated theses constitute the framework:

1. **Triadic Reality Architecture** -- Three irreducible co-primitives (L3, I-infinity, A) ground physics
2. **The Ground of the Architecture** -- The triadic structure requires a ground with specific functional roles
3. **Creational Rationality** -- Rational structure is the expression of the ground, not accidental or emergent
4. **Approximation Theory of Finite Reason** -- Finite rational systems approximate rather than generate rational structure
5. **Person-Artifact Distinction** -- Persons participate in rational order categorically differently from artifacts
6. **Wisdom as Ordered Grasp** -- Disciplined apprehension of the ontic order

## Papers

1. **Core Thesis** (`papers/core-thesis/`) -- The founding paper establishing Ologosophy's six theses and triadic architecture
2. **Christ as Logos** (`papers/christ-as-logos/`) -- Companion paper arguing the christological articulation is the most explanatorily adequate specification of the Logos ground, drawing on Catholic, Orthodox, and Reformed traditions
3. **Ologosophical AI** (`papers/ologosophical-ai/`) -- Philosophy of artificial intelligence within the Ologosophical framework: derivative knowledge thesis, ontological alignment, Turing oracle identification, PCLR architecture, and reframed AI ethics
